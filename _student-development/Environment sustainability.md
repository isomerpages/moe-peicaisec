<h4><strong>Environment</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Objectives:</strong>
</p>
<p>As part of the Singapore Green Plan 2030, all MOE schools have embarked
on the Eco Stewardship Programme (ESP) since 2021. It adopts a whole-school
approach to integrating environmental sustainability, in nurturing stewards
of the environment and aims to reduce carbon emissions by two-thirds as
a school sector by 2030, with at least 20% of our schools to be carbon
neutral.</p>
<p>The Eco Stewardship Programme builds on current environmental efforts
in schools. This will be done holistically through the 4Cs:</p>
<p>● Curriculum: Enhance teaching and learning of sustainability in Science
and Humanities, and integrate sustainability features into learning resources
to make learning more authentic
<br>● Campus: Enhance campus with sustainability features like energy-efficient
LED lights
<br>● Culture: Practise daily habits like reducing food waste and energy use,
and encourage students to champion sound sustainability practices amongst
peers
<br>● Community: Leverage community partnerships to offer students more environment-related
learning opportunities</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Whole School Approach for ESP@Peicai</strong>
</p>
<p>In Peicai Secondary School, the ESP was officially rolled out in 2023
where environmental programmes and activities are designed to meet the
different needs of the students with support from various stakeholders.
Below shows a summary of the whole school approach for ESP@Peicai.</p>
<p>Every Peicaian will have the opportunity to learn and participate in environment
programmes through Tier 1 experiences such as the mass celebration of environmental
days such as World Water Day and Earth Day, assembly programmes as well
as exhibitions in the foyer. They can also participate in school-wide recycling
programmes such as “SembEnviro Plastic Recycling League” and the “Pass-it-on
Bazaar”.</p>
<h4><strong>Key Programmes:</strong></h4>
<p><strong>2026 Earth Day</strong>
</p>
<p>In commemoration of Earth Day, our school began the week with a meaningful
morning sharing to raise awareness about the importance of environmental
stewardship. Led by our Environment Head representatives, the sharing introduced
the significance of Earth Day and this year’s theme, “Our Power, Our Planet,”
which highlights the role that individuals and communities play in protecting
the environment through everyday actions. Students were encouraged to reflect
on simple yet impactful habits, such as reducing waste, conserving water,
and making more environmentally conscious decisions in their daily lives.
The sharing served as a timely reminder that collective action begins with
individual responsibility, and that every effort, no matter how small,
contributes to a larger impact.</p>
<p>To reinforce these messages, an activity corner was set up in the library
in collaboration with PCSS English Language and Literature department.
The corner featured a bookmark-making activity alongside informative posters
that highlighted practical ways students can adopt more environmentally
friendly habits. Using recycled materials, students created personalised
bookmarks, reinforcing the idea of giving new purpose to items that would
otherwise be discarded.</p>
<p>Through these activities, students not only deepened their understanding
of sustainability but also took a meaningful step towards becoming more
responsible and environmentally conscious members of the community.</p>

<p><strong>&nbsp;Sec 2 Learning Journey to Treelodge@Punggol</strong>
</p>
<p>In Term 2, the Humanities and Science Departments organised an interdisciplinary
learning journey to Treelodge@Punggol for our Secondary 2 students. As
Singapore’s first eco-precinct designed to harness natural elements to
promote sustainable living, Treelodge@Punggol provided a meaningful platform
for students to explore how urban planning can respond to environmental
challenges such as climate change.</p>
<p>During the visit, students engaged in on-site observations and participated
in a series of activities across four stations at the Eco-Deck. Through
these activities, they examined how sustainable housing design and the
thoughtful planning of amenities can help reduce environmental impact while
supporting residents’ well-being. They also applied key Science concepts
such as energy transfer and heat management to understand how features
like natural ventilation, urban greenery and energy-efficient systems contribute
to lowering heat, conserving resources and mitigating the effects of urbanisation.</p>
<p>Beyond developing conceptual understanding, the learning journey encouraged
students to reflect on their role as stewards of the environment. By observing
how sustainable practices are embedded in everyday living spaces, students
gained a deeper awareness of how individual and collective actions can
contribute to building a more climate-resilient and sustainable Singapore.</p>

<p><strong>VIA projects related to sustainability:</strong>
</p>
<p>As part of our school’s commitment to nurturing active and responsible
citizens, students participated in a Values-in-Action (VIA) programme that
addressed a range of social causes. Across different classes, students
engaged in diverse projects, with environmental sustainability being one
of the key focus areas. In this area, students engaged in a range of initiatives,
including beach clean-ups and campaigns to raise awareness, allowing them
to contribute meaningfully to environmental protection in different ways.</p>
<p>Some classes participated in beach clean-ups, where students worked collaboratively
to collect and sort litter along the shoreline. Beyond the physical task,
the experience prompted them to reflect on the importance of reducing,
reusing and recycling as part of a sustainable lifestyle. They explored
how improper waste disposal impacts marine life, disrupts ecosystems and
contributes to broader environmental challenges such as climate change.
This hands-on experience helped students better understand the impact of
human activities on the environment and the importance of individual responsibility
in driving change.</p>
<p>Other classes focused on raising awareness within the school community
by designing posters and campaigns to promote environmentally responsible
habits, such as reducing single-use plastics and practising proper waste
disposal. Through these efforts, students not only deepened their understanding
of environmental issues but also took on the role of advocates for change,
encouraging their peers to adopt more sustainable practices.</p>
<p>Through the various VIA projects, students reflected on important values
such as responsibility, care and respect for the environment. They recognised
that protecting the environment is a shared responsibility, and that even
small, individual actions can collectively make a meaningful difference.</p>

<p><strong>Climate Crisis Challenge 2025</strong>
</p>
<p>The Climate Change Challenge (CCC) is a nationwide competition that encourages
secondary school students to create innovative solutions for Singapore’s
environmental resilience and global sustainability. It offers valuable
opportunities to enhance knowledge, teamwork, problem-solving, and presentation
skills while fostering entrepreneurship and creative thinking to tackle
climate change.</p>

<p><strong><u>2024</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Singapore Youth Water Conference 2024</strong>
</p>
<p>Peicai Environment Heads participated in the Singapore Youth Water Conference
2024, organized by Raffles Institution. Titled “Singapore’s Water Future:
Challenges and Opportunities for the Next Generation,” the forum emphasized
the importance of water sustainability and management practices, such as
desalination and NEWater, which Singapore uses to address water scarcity.
The conference also highlighted future challenges like climate change and
the need for proactive solutions to ensure water security. Key topics included
the role of community involvement, youth engagement, and the use of technology,
such as drones and AI, in achieving water conservation goals.</p>
<p>Following the conference, our Environment Heads shared these insights
with their schoolmates during an assembly. They called for students to
adopt new water-saving practices and facilitated discussions on how the
school community can improve its water conservation efforts.</p>
