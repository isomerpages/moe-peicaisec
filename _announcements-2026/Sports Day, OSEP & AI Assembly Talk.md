<p>The assembly began with a Sports Briefing by Mr Alvin, HOD of PE &amp;
CCA, who shared key details about the upcoming Sports Day. He outlined
the programme and expectations, building anticipation among students. He
also introduced the Guest of Honour, <strong>Shanti Pereira</strong>, a <strong>national sprinter</strong> whose
sporting achievements, discipline and dedication continue to inspire young
people to pursue excellence and resilience.</p>

<p>This was followed by a sharing on the Overseas School Exchange Programme
(OSEP) by Mr James Koh, HOD of School Partnership and Outreach. He introduced
the four overseas destinations that students will be embarking on this
year—Japan, Thailand, Hong Kong and China (Beijing). The programme promises
to be an exciting and enriching journey, offering students valuable exposure
to different cultures, education systems and global perspectives.</p>

<p>The assembly concluded with an engaging AI talk by Ms Zhou Wensi, Senior
Teacher of History, who explored the role of Artificial Intelligence in
today’s interconnected world. She emphasised the importance of using AI
responsibly and ethically, particularly in learning contexts. The session
was further enriched by interviews with teachers and students, who shared
their perspectives on how AI can support learning while underscoring the
importance of critical thinking and discernment.</p>
<p>Overall, the assembly offered students meaningful insights into upcoming
events and future-ready learning, encouraging them to stay engaged, broaden
their perspectives and use technology responsibly.</p>
