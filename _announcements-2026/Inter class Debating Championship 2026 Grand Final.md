<p>The school hall came alive with spirited discussion and persuasive speaking
on Monday as students gathered for the Inter-Class Debating Championship.
The championship featured an exciting face-off between <strong>3EE</strong>,
representing the Proposition, and <strong>3CE</strong>, representing the
Opposition, as they debated the motion: <em>"This House Believes that Artificial Intelligence (AI) will improve humanity more than harm it."</em> For
the purposes of the debate, "humanity" was defined as the qualities that
make us human.</p>
<p>&nbsp;Both teams demonstrated impressive critical thinking, confident
public speaking, and the ability to engage thoughtfully with one of today's
most pressing issues. Speakers presented well-reasoned arguments, challenged
opposing viewpoints with insightful rebuttals, and responded effectively
under pressure, reflecting the high standard of preparation and teamwork
displayed throughout the competition.</p>
<p>&nbsp;A highlight of the championship was the interactive Question-and-Answer
segment, also known as the Points of Clarification, where both teachers
and students were invited to pose questions related to the motion. Thinking
on their feet, the debaters responded spontaneously to the audience's challenges,
showcasing their depth of knowledge, quick thinking, and composure under
pressure.</p>
<p>After a closely contested debate, <strong>3EE</strong> emerged as the winning
team, successfully convincing the adjudicators that the benefits of artificial
intelligence outweigh its potential drawbacks. Individual excellence was
also recognised, with the <strong>Best Speaker</strong> award going to a
member of the <strong>3CE</strong> Opposition team for an outstanding display
of eloquence, analysis, and rebuttal.</p>
<p>The championship was a celebration of intellectual curiosity, respectful
discourse, and confident communication—hallmarks of the school's commitment
to nurturing thoughtful, articulate, and future-ready learners.</p>
