<h4>2026</h4>
<p>We are proud to announce that <strong>MSG (NCC) Ng Yee Shan (4PN)</strong> has
been awarded the <strong>National Cadet Corps (NCC) Outstanding Cadet Award</strong>,
one of the highest honours presented to outstanding NCC cadets.</p>
<p>&nbsp;The <strong>NCC Outstanding Cadet Award</strong> is a prestigious
accolade conferred upon outstanding Senior Cadet Leaders who have demonstrated
exceptional leadership qualities, inspired their peers, and served as exemplary
role models within their units. The award was presented to Yee Shan on <strong>11 July</strong>,
in the presence of her family, friends and fellow cadets, in recognition
of her exemplary leadership and unwavering commitment to the Corps.</p>
<p>&nbsp;As the <strong>Assistant Sergeant Major</strong> of our school's NCC
unit, Yee Shan has consistently exemplified these qualities through her
dedication, discipline, and commitment to mentoring and supporting her
juniors. Her positive influence and steadfast service have made a lasting
contribution to the unit.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<em>"This milestone is not the end of your journey, but the beginning of a new chapter as you step into your next phase of growth and service."</em> — <strong>Commandant NCC</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>We extend our heartfelt congratulations to Yee
Shan on this remarkable achievement. Her accomplishment reflects the values
of leadership, resilience and service that our school strives to nurture.
We are confident that she will continue to lead with integrity, serve with
humility, and achieve even greater success in her future endeavours.</p>

<p>On 22 May 2026, our NCC (Sea) Unit traded their boots for paddles as they
took to the waters for the highly anticipated Dragon Boat Regatta. Competing
against some of the finest teams from across the nation, our cadets showcased
an incredible display of synchronicity, mental fortitude, and sheer physical
endurance.
<br>
<br>We are immensely proud to announce that our intensive training regimen
bore fruit in a major way: both of our school’s registered boats beat their
previous personal bests, achieving significant improvements in their race
timings (Personal best timing 1:07:71). In a stunning display of power
and teamwork, one of our boats successfully fought its way through the
intense heats to secure a <strong>well-deserved spot in the Semi-Finals</strong>.
Watching our cadets row in perfect harmony, cutting through the water with
determination in every stroke, was an absolute inspiration for everyone
cheering from the riverbanks.
<br>
<br>While medals may be awarded on the podium, character is forged through
perseverance in the water. The resilience demonstrated by our cadets—pressing
on despite physical fatigue and challenging their own limits—reflects the
true spirit and values of the National Cadet Corps. Their determination,
discipline, and unwavering commitment in the face of adversity exemplify
the qualities of exemplary cadets and serve as a testament to their strength
of character.</p>
<p>Congratulations to all our dragon boat racers!</p>

<p>The Debate Society achieved an outstanding result at the Raffles Institution
Under-14s Debating Championship 2026, emerging as Champions of the Silver
Division. Organised by Raffles Institution, the prestigious tournament
brought together talented young debaters from schools across Singapore,
providing a platform for participants to engage critically with contemporary
issues and develop their public speaking skills.</p>
<p>The championship aimed to cultivate critical thinking, effective communication,
collaboration, and adaptability among participants. Through a series of
challenging debate rounds, students were required to analyse complex motions,
construct persuasive arguments, rebut opposing viewpoints, and respond
confidently under pressure. The competition also provided valuable opportunities
for students to interact with peers from different schools and broaden
their perspectives on a range of social, political, and ethical issues.</p>
<p>Representing the school were members of the Debate Society, who demonstrated
remarkable resilience, teamwork, and intellectual rigour throughout the
tournament. Their commitment to extensive preparation and continuous improvement
enabled them to perform consistently well against strong opposition teams.</p>
<p>One of the highlights of the championship was the highly competitive elimination
rounds, where students showcased their ability to think critically and
communicate persuasively under time constraints. The team impressed adjudicators
with their depth of analysis, clarity of expression, and effective teamwork.
Their hard work and determination culminated in a stellar performance in
the final round, where they secured the championship title in the Silver
Division.</p>
<p>This achievement reflects the students’ dedication to excellence and their
strong mastery of debate skills. The success also highlights the Debate
Society’s commitment to nurturing confident, articulate, and globally aware
communicators who are prepared to engage thoughtfully with the world around
them.</p>

<p><strong>Peicai D&amp;T Team Programmes Their Way to Victory at ignITE X-Bots Challenge</strong>
</p>
<p>We are pleased to share that our Secondary 3 Design &amp; Technology students
emerged as Champions at the ignITE X-Bots Challenge Finals, held on 26
May at ITE College East.</p>
<p>The team comprised three Secondary 3 D&amp;T students:
<br>• Chew Yong Xiang Keith, 3CE
<br>• Ong Jingkai Ashton, 3PN
<br>• Yu Yee Fei Evelyn, 3PN</p>
<p>The students competed against 10 other teams in the finals and rose admirably
to the challenge. Their journey began on 3 April with their first workshop,
where they were introduced to robotics concepts, coding and hands-on tasks.
Through the process, the team demonstrated strong commitment, independence
and teamwork as they prepared for the competition.
<br>
<br>At the preliminary round on 16 April, the team had to apply their problem-solving
skills under time constraints. Despite facing technical challenges, they
remained composed, worked together to troubleshoot the issues and successfully
qualified for the finals.</p>
<p>During the finals on 26 May, the team worked collaboratively to programme
and refine their robot for the competition tasks. Their perseverance, focus
and effective teamwork enabled them to perform well and clinch the championship
title.</p>
<p>This achievement is a proud moment for Peicai Secondary School and reflects
our students’ creativity, resilience and growing confidence in applying
STEM-related skills beyond the classroom.</p>
<p>We congratulate Keith, Ashton and Evelyn on their outstanding achievement,
and thank their teachers for guiding and supporting them throughout this
learning journey.</p>

<p>On 28 April 2026, three teams of Elements of Business Skills (EBS) students,
each comprising three members, participated in the preliminary round of
the EBS Online Competition conducted via the Student Learning Space (SLS).
Competing against more than 30 participating schools through inquiry-based
tasks, all nine of our students performed commendably and successfully
advanced to the final round. The final round, held on 19 May 2026, took
the form of an Amazing Race challenge across the Esplanade, Marina Square,
and CityLink Mall. Our students were tasked with applying their understanding
of the travel and tourism, hospitality, and retail industries in authentic
real-world contexts.
<br>
<br>Through a series of dynamic challenges, they analysed business scenarios,
solved problems under tight time constraints, and adapted quickly to unfamiliar
situations. The competition provided our students with valuable opportunities
to strengthen key 21st Century Competencies such as critical and adaptive
thinking, communication, and collaboration. Working closely in teams, our
students demonstrated initiative, effective teamwork, and the ability to
make informed decisions in fast-paced environments.
<br>
<br>Beyond the competition itself, the experience enabled our students to
deepen their learning beyond the classroom, forge stronger bonds with their
teammates, and boost confidence by overcoming challenges together. It was
a meaningful and enriching experience that encouraged them to step out
of their comfort zones while developing important life skills. We are proud
to announce that one of our teams emerged as the <strong>Second Runner-Up</strong> in
the Amazing Race competition. In addition, Elysa Lim Qing was awarded the <strong>individual accolade of Best in Accommodation</strong> for
her outstanding performance.</p>

<p><strong>Art Club CCA – Singapore Youth Festival 2026 Arts Presentation</strong>
</p>
<p>We are thrilled to announce that our Art Club CCA has achieved a remarkable
milestone at the Singapore Youth Festival (SYF) 2026 Arts Presentation.
This year, SYF commemorates its 60th anniversary under the inspiring theme <em>'SYF 60: The ArtsXPerience - Inspire, Connect, Empower'</em>,
a celebration that recognises students' artistic growth and the creative
processes that foster innovation, build meaningful connections, and empower
young people to make a positive impact through the arts.
<br>
<br>The 2026 exhibition theme, <em>'Artist and XPerience'</em>, invited students
to reflect on their personal artistic journeys - &nbsp;the breakthrough
moments, happy accidents, and hard-won discoveries that shape each artist's
evolving creative voice. It is a theme that speaks to the heart of what
art-making truly is, not merely the final piece on display, but the entire
process of exploration, experimentation, and self-discovery that leads
to it.
<br>
<br>We are proud to share that in this highly competitive showcase, four of
our Art Club members rose to the occasion and produced works of exceptional
quality and depth. Their artworks have each been awarded the Certificate
of Recognition and selected for the on-site exhibition, which is a testament
to their dedication, creativity, and growth as young artists.
<br>
<br>Their works will be displayed at the National Gallery Singapore from 4
to 19 July 2026. This is a wonderful opportunity to witness the talent
and passion that our Art Club members have poured into their creative journeys.
We warmly invite all Peicaians to visit the exhibition and show your support
for your fellow schoolmates. Let us celebrate their achievements together
and be inspired by the stories their art has to tell.</p>

<h4>Volleyball Girls C Division NSG League 3 Runners Up 2025</h4>
<p>This year, our C Division Volleyball Girls Team achieved a commendable
milestone by qualifying for the League 3 Finals at the National School
Games (NSG). They have broken a nearly 30 year wait for a medal ever since
we were featured in the North Finals back in 1995.</p>
<p>The National School Games (NSG) is the largest annual inter-school competition
in Singapore, organised by schools for schools. It is a highly anticipated
event in the sporting calendar, offering more than 400 championship titles
to over 60,000 student athletes across primary schools, secondary schools,
junior colleges, and centralised institutions. The NSG not only provides
opportunities for healthy competition, but also cultivates values such
as resilience, teamwork, and sportsmanship.</p>
<p>Throughout the NSG competitions, the girls displayed unwavering grit and
determination, training hard and giving their very best in every match.
In the Finals, they put up an inspiring fight against Queenstown Secondary,
playing with heart and perseverance, before finishing as League 3 Runners-up.</p>
<p>This journey in preparing for the NSG reflects the spirit of commitment
and teamwork, and the school is proud of the girls’ accomplishment. Thank
you to the school leaders, teachers-in-charge, and the coaches for their
guidance and steadfast support throughout the season, and to the girls
for proudly flying our school flag high.</p>

<h4>Girl Guides World Thinking Day Commemoration</h4>
<p><strong><u>Peicai Girl Guides</u></strong>
</p>
<p>On February 22nd, schools across the world celebrate World Thinking Day,
a special occasion when Girl Guides and Brownies come together to honour
international friendship and raise awareness on issues affecting girls
and young women.</p>
<p>The Theme for World Thinking Day this year is Our Story. Reflecting on
the symbolism and values of our Movement, “Our Story” explores what it
has meant to be a Girl Guide over the years. This includes exploring how
Girl Guides can understand the past in the world today, while supporting
girls around the world to feel confident, care about their world and develop
the skills needed to make a difference.</p>
<p>This year, Brownies and Girl Guides gathered at the Singapore Expo to
celebrate World Thinking Day (WTD). This special occasion celebrates our
global movement, strengthens international friendships, and shares the
inspiring stories of our Guiding community. We were also honoured to have
Ms. Candela Gonzalez, Chair of the World Board, WAGGGS, as our Guest of
Honour at the special occasion.</p>
<p>In our school, the Girl Guides marked World Thinking Day during the morning
assembly by watching an inspiring video message from Dr. Seetha Subbiah,
the President of Girl Guides Singapore. This observance was a powerful
reminder for everyone to reflect on their role in creating a more inclusive
and just world for girls and young women.</p>
