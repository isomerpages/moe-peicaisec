<p><strong><u>General Questions</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Q1.</strong> The uniform supplier does not have the uniform size
of my child. Can my child come to school in his primary school uniform?</p>
<p><strong>A1.</strong> If the uniform supplier does not have your child’s
required size, please submit a pre-order with the supplier. Alternatively,
you may choose to have the uniform tailor-made externally if the supplier
is unable to provide that size.</p>
<p>In the meantime, your child may attend school in Peicai Secondary School
PE attire (if available). If not, he/she may continue to wear his/her primary
school uniform or PE attire until the Peicai uniform is ready.</p>
<p><strong><u>Mother Tongue</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Q1.</strong> Why is Tamil Language not offered at Peicai Secondary?</p>
<p><strong>A1.</strong> Similar to some other schools, due to the small size
of the cohort, resources are maximised at designated TL centres.</p>
<p><strong>Q2.</strong> Where do students go for Tamil Language lessons?</p>
<p><strong>A2.</strong> Students who offered to study TL, will need to attend
TL lessons at designated TL centres. For Peicai Secondary School, the designated
TL centre by MOE is at Umar Pulavar Tamil Language Centre (UPTLC). Please
refer to their website at <a href="https://www.uptlc.moe.edu.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.uptlc.moe.edu.sg</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Q3.</strong> Does Peicai Secondary offer Higher Mother Tongue?</p>
<p><strong>A3.</strong> Yes. Peicai Secondary offers Higher Chinese/Higher
Malay at Secondary One. However, Higher Tamil students will attend lessons
at Umar Pulavar Tamil Language Centre (UPTLC). Please refer to their website
at <a href="https://www.uptlc.moe.edu.sg" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.uptlc.moe.edu.sg</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Q4.</strong> Are lessons for TL and HTL at the centres conducted
during curriculum time?</p>
<p><strong>A4. </strong>No. It is conducted outside curriculum time. Depending
on the centre, timing and curriculum days will vary. While in school, your
child/ward will have independent self-study during the MTL periods in the
school library or other assigned venue.</p>
<p><strong>Q5.</strong> What happens if lessons at the designated centres
outside Peicai Secondary School clashes with CCA or remedial lessons in
school?</p>
<p><strong>A5.</strong> Activities/Lessons in school will take precedence.
Special arrangements will be made where necessary. Students are to alert
teachers in charge of any potential clashes.</p>
<p><strong>Q6.</strong> What will be the arrangement for my child/ward if
he/she is offering Non-Tamil Indian Languages (Hindi/Gujarati/Bengali/Panjabi/Urdu)
or Asian/Foreign Languages?</p>
<p><strong>A6.</strong> Your child will continue to attend the lessons at
their respective centre or institutions. While in school, your child/ward
will have independent self-study during the MTL periods in the school library
or other assigned venue.</p>
<p><strong><u>CCA – General</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Q1.</strong> Is it compulsory for all students to have a CCA?</p>
<p><strong>A1.</strong> Yes, it is compulsory for all secondary school students
to have at least one CCA in school.</p>
<p><strong>Q2.</strong> If my child has an external CCA, is he/she allowed
to be excused from CCA in school?</p>
<p><strong>A2.</strong> No, it is compulsory for ALL students to have at least
one CCA in school. However, students/parents may request to participate
in inter-school competitions in the name of the school for the external
CCA. For example, Peicai Secondary School does not offer Taekwondo. However,
students/parents may request to represent Peicai and participate in the
inter-school Taekwondo competition. Note that the joint-cluster schools
CCA for Table Tennis (Boys) is considered as a CCA offered by Peicai Secondary
School rather than an external CCA.</p>
<p><strong>Q3.</strong> What are the CCAs offered in Peicai?</p>
<p><strong>A3.</strong> Please refer to the school website for information
at the following link</p>
<p><a href="https://www.peicaisec.moe.edu.sg/cca/ccas/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.peicaisec.moe.edu.sg/cca/ccas/</a>
</p>
<p><strong>Q4.</strong> Which is the CCA day in Peicai?</p>
<p><strong>A4.</strong> CCAs are scheduled to take place on Mondays, Tuesdays
and Thursdays. Different CCAs could be on different days and the frequency
of CCA sessions could also differ from CCAs to CCAs.</p>
<p>There are no CCAs scheduled on Wednesdays and Fridays. However, sessions
may be scheduled on Fridays during the peak competition periods where necessary.</p>
<p><strong>Q5.</strong> How frequent are CCA sessions?</p>
<p><strong>A5.</strong> There is no fixed frequency for CCA sessions. Depending
on the nature of CCAs, the frequency varies from once to twice a week.
It is important for students and parents to note the frequency before selecting
the CCA preferences during the Sec 1 CCA Allocation window. Do note that
during certain seasons, students may require more training sessions to
prepare for the matches/performances, this will include National Schools
Games, Singapore Youth Festival Art Presentation, key regional events within
the Uniformed Groups, Clubs and Societies calendar, etc. Parents will be
informed via PG by the respective CCA Teacher-In-Charge.</p>
<p><strong><u>CCA Open House 2026</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Q1.</strong> Will there be face-to-face sessions where students
get to mingle with the current CCA students?</p>
<p><strong>A1.</strong> Yes, there will be a CCA Open House during the Sec
1 Parent Engagement Session, on 17 January 2026 (Sat). Parents who are
present can also check in with our CCA Teachers for more details about
CCAs offered.</p>
<p><strong>Q2.</strong> Is there a briefing on how CCAs can be selected?</p>
<p><strong>A2.</strong> Yes, during the CCA Open House, parents and students
will be briefed on the steps for CCA selection.</p>
<p><strong>Q3.</strong> Will the new students get to watch performances by
the Performing Arts (PA) Groups?</p>
<p><strong>A3.</strong> Yes, students will visit the allocated venues of these
CCAs during the CCA Open House and watch a short performance prepared by
the PA-CCAs.</p>
<p><strong>Q4.</strong> Is it compulsory for all students and parents to attend
the CCA Open House?</p>
<p><strong>A4.</strong> It is compulsory for all Sec 1 students. Parents are
strongly encouraged to accompany their children to attend the CCA Open
House.</p>
<p><strong>Q5.</strong> Will there be a make-up of the CCA Open House if students/parents
miss it?</p>
<p><strong>A5.</strong> No, there will not be any make-up session. Sec 1 students
can still approach CCA teachers during term time to enquire more, before
the final submission deadline for CCA preferences on 23 January 2026 (Fri).</p>
<p><strong><u>Sec 1 Orientation Week 2026</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Q1.</strong> Is it compulsory for all Sec 1 students to attend
the Sec 1 Orientation Week?</p>
<p><strong>A1.</strong> Yes. Sec 1 Orientation Week is a milestone programme
for all Sec 1 students who enter the school as it will be the first platform
for them to get to know their classmates, school and teachers.</p>
<p><strong>Q2.</strong> What is the purpose of the Sec 1 Orientation Week?</p>
<p><strong>A2.</strong> Sec 1 Orientation is the first platform for Sec 1s
to be more familiar with their classmates, teachers and the school. Students
build closer bonds within the class and a stronger class and school identity.
Through Sec 1 Orientation, students will be better assimilated into Peicai
Secondary School and will have a greater sense of belonging and pride to
the school. During the programme, students will also be introduced to new
secondary level subjects, including Humanities, Design &amp; Technology,
and the Applied Learning Programme, and be equipped with coping strategies
that support a smooth transition from primary to secondary school.</p>
<p><strong>Q3.</strong> Can my child go off earlier during the Sec 1 Orientation
Week?</p>
<p><strong>A3.</strong> All Sec 1s are expected to be present throughout the
duration of the programme. Please refer to the orientation programme for
the dismissal time for each day during the orientation week. Kindly inform
your child’s form teachers should there be a need to leave school earlier
on a specific day for a valid reason.</p>
